# Chapter 5: The Message

Shen woke up to the sound of beating drums. It thudded like the foot stomps of a primeval beast with a simple 1-2-1 rhythm. [More descriptions] He shuddered.

The morning light grazed his eyes, and he half-heartedly opened his eyes in reluctance to meet the morning sun. He had forgotten to close the blinds again and this was what rewarded him. He could feel the fog of sleep lifting from him. With this growing clarity, Shen realized that there were no drumbeats. All that was just the thudding pain from a migraine in his head that seemed to gestate into notable rhythms.

“What time is it?” he muttered to the empty room.

The silence was frightening. He half-turned with a vain hope that she was still beside him. Of course, she wasn’t. She was in space on the way to Nexus 1. A one-way suicide trip he had thought and tried to warn her to stay away. Of course, she never listened. The love between two humans though dynamically changing and possibly growing could never measure up a deep love of someone’s passion in exploration and intellectual pursuit.

He sighed and voice activated the TV. The news came on. An anchor, no doubt a latest 5th generation AI avatar, spoke resolutely about the on-going resource war in Peru and Bolivia. What was mentioned was the on-going fight between the two countries over resources and control of the mines. More villagers and towns as statistical numbers were quoted. Some images and videos were shown of the aftermath of the carnage. No illegal weaponry were used with UN Peacekeepers monitoring but conventional approved weaponry were enough these days to cause significant bloodshed.

Shen scoffed. The news these days was worthless. What was not mentioned between the lines was that all the mines on Earth and Luna were running low on resources. Mars alone did not have enough to continue sending the amount necessary to support the 12 trillion people in the Earth-Luna system not even including all the mostly self-sustaining orbital space stations. The technology to mine asteroids have been around for nearly a century now but only now with resource wars happening were visionaries finally getting the funds needed to explore how to do them economically.

Another sign of the short-sightedness of humanity. No one truly cared when the scientists and environmental activist groups warned that the ocean levels were rising every year with increasing temperature until major cities in the powerful nations of the world were starting to be submerged underwater. 

Shen mused aloud. “People can barely see past their fingertips. Maybe this is the message that these aliens are coming here to help us with. To learn how to be better stewards of ourselves and this solar system before eventually we reach a crisis that is too late to resolve.”

He turned off the TV and glanced at the unfinished book laying on the top of a stack of books. It was a poetry book which he started ten years ago that never transpired into fully published form. He grabbed it and flipped to a random page. The page number read 123. The poem was entitled “Wilderness of My Mind”. It was short. He read the next poem called “Orbits”.

Shen wondered what happened to his penchant in writing poetry. It seemed to have vanished over the years as morning fog disappeared in the stillness of bright sunlight. He had thought of writing a science fiction novel that wrapped around the poetry but never took the step down to put pen to paper. He was too afraid of failure and judgment to try. Also, maybe he was too comfortable writing what he was used to despite it offering diminishing returns on meaning for him. 

He wondered how authors like Ted Chiang did it. The guy has a computer science degree but ended up writing novellas and short stories that were truly transformative and informative. They were the type of works that got people thinking about what the future could possibly look like or how technology could impact us in a very human sense. The story “Story of Your Life” of learning to communicate with an alien intelligence with the help of a human linguist was in some ways akin to this one he was living in.

Ted Chiang had never written a novel at the time he published that work. Perhaps it’s not the quantity of words but the quality of ideas presented. Shen felt like he had a lot of ideas but most just stayed in his head. If presented in the real world, it would be subjected to scrutiny and either stolen or bashed to pieces. It was safer to stay in his head.

“If I ever do write a novel, I would never be one of those authors who would self-insert themselves into one,” Shen thought.

The irony of this would be surprising to Shen later. He didn’t think any of that would be in this current book, mostly since he thought Apoapsis would have long been completed by then. There was a brief spurt of time at end of January that year where he was writing a lot. Then he lost his steam. Why was the notion that writing would lead him nowhere. He had told a poet friend of his that he stopped writing fiction or poetry since he didn’t feel like it would achieve anything for him professionally. Ironically, this was despite the fact that there were writing workshops, notably one where Ted Chiang participated in, which he felt that he wanted to submit some works and get accepted to as well.

Sometimes, the journey to somewhere starts with prep work. One might not think one is moving but one actually is! Just that the journey itself might require a chunk of time in prepping to actually get on-board. For instance, getting selected for a writing workshop without paying exorbitant fees meant that he actually had to produce enough works of value to be able to submit. That particular writing workshop required three original works of science fiction short stories. He did not have anything ready to go in the form of short stories for submission but if he had written more beforehand, he would have had a volume to choose from by the time of submission deadline. Since this was once a year, he decided just now that he would like to write more science fiction this year in preparation for that workshop and any other one.

So what changed on this particular day on April 13, 2026? He visited his family living in Atlanta, Georgia in April 2026. Now, neither of his parents would give him any unconventional career advice. This came from grabbing a random book on the bookshelf that was no doubt left by his *meimei* (younger sister) who was a massive bibliophile. The particular book called *The Art of Non-conformity* by Chris Guillebeau informed the reader on stories of people living unconventional lives and discovering their purpose. In there, it mentioned the book *Wishcraft* by Barabara Sher who stated that “Whatever your dreams are, start taking them very, very seriously.”

The author himself stated that at one point he was having a lot of fun and doing many different things, he was not taking his real dreams seriously. This Shen decided was his case as well. If he still dreams of being an established writer of some repute, why would he stop writing?

The idea that other things were more important than pursuing personal dreams was a form of self-delusion. It was holding the self back from the fear of failure or the notion that other things in life was more important. Though coding up a videogame might sound more important, writing prose or poetry was also very essential to his core being.

Hence, this was why two months after the last update on February 14, 2026, he picked up his metaphorical pen and decided to continue writing the story despite his worry that much of it might end up being drivel with a discombobulated plot. 

After a short while he stopped and took a break. He went downstairs to get a drink of water from the kitchen. While there, he noticed a picture on the wall. It was one that was given to him by his ex five years ago. *Why do I still have that thing?* He suddenly felt a pang of loneliness and a desire for both emotional and physical intimacy. The right thing to do would be to not dwell and get back to writing. However, the allure of the virtual reality (VR) pod was strong.

This era has far surpassed the Internet capability of the early 2000s in terms of mediavisual production. This was past the stages of the early VR goggles or glasses. Instead of bulky headsets that only provides video and audio and the occasional motion sickness, technology these days in the VR front composed of wearing a haptic suit that covers the entire body with a small amount of space for breathing through the nose. The suit provides full realism in every sense except for taste. That would require a neural implant which Shen was not willing to go there. There are people who were willing to become cyborgs of a sort but he wasn't one of them.

What was even more alluring was the possibility of using AI to create people from images loaded. The sophistication of modern AI was enough for images of people long gone or past their prime to be loaded up and created as hyperrealistic people (with a minimal base set of uploaded personalities). The nerds of high schools these days who would never get the popular girl would only have to scour online social media for enough images to then dump them into an AI avatar generator and create the girlfriend of their dreams. And assuming they have a VR haptic suit which costed about $20K, they essentially can buy their perfect girlfriend.

People didn't seem to care about the lawsuits that inevitably developed from improper use of others' likeness. It was a nightmare scenario for a lot of people who had to be careful what they put online lest entire avatars were created of them and used in ways they never intended. What technology has wrought was both a demon and an angel. Shen himself would never do this on anyone who was still alive since that felt like a sense of severe boundary intrusion.

He got into his suit and loaded up a model from the early 21st century. The 3D representation was generated from him loading up hundreds of images of this woman through one of the most sophisticated AI girlfriend generation models in existence. The personality was loaded to be a charming elegant woman. There were some brief conversation but then he was in the action. She looked nothing like his ex which was what he wanted. The part of his mind that stated this was all just an illusion he ignored thoroughly. He also didn't want to think about who this woman was in real life back in the last century.

The moaning and gyration went on for a while. In his suit, he heard the woman cry out his name and he cried out her name as well. He would have to carefully scrub the suit again after this mess.
